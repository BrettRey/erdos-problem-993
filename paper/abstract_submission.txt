Alavi, Malde, Schwenk, and Erdős (1987) conjectured that the independence polynomial of every tree is unimodal. I prove $\mu(T) < n/3$ for every $d_{\mathrm{leaf}} \le 1$ tree on $n \ge 3$ vertices via Steiner peeling on a compensation function. This mean bound would settle Conjecture A if tree independence polynomials satisfied $\mathrm{mode} \le \lceil\mu\rceil$; that tree-specific mode--mean inequality remains open. A subdivision-contraction identity shows that, conditional on a conjectured Edge Contraction Mode Stability property and a combined tail condition verified through $n \le 19$, any minimal counterexample is homeomorphically irreducible. Neither conditional step closes the full conjecture. Computationally, I verify unimodality for all 8,691,747,673 trees on $n \le 29$ vertices. Multi-arm stars are the empirically extremal family in the tested regimes, with near-miss ratio $\mathrm{nm}(s) = 1 - C/s + O(1/s^2)$ along each parity class, where the parity-dependent constant satisfies $4 \le C \le 8$.
