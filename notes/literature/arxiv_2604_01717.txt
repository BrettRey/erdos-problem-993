                                                                                       Abstract
                                                      The hard-core model can be used to understand the number of independent sets in
                                                  graphs in extremal graph theory. The occupancy fraction, defined as the logarithmic
                                                  derivative of the independence polynomial of a graph, is a key quantity in hard-core
                                                  model. Davies et al. (2017) established an upper bound on the occupancy fraction for
                                                  d-regular graphs, and Perarnau and Perkins (2018) derived a corresponding bound on
                                                  it for graphs with given girth. Inspired by their work, we provide the tight upper and
                                                  lower bounds on occupancy fraction in n-vertex graphs with independence number خ±,
                                                  extending the classical results on bounds for independence polynomials. We also prove
                                                  a relevant conjecture posed by Davies et al. (2025) on this topic.
                                                  Keywords: Independent set; Independence polynomial; Hard-core model;
                                                  Occupancy fraction.
                                                  AMS Classification (2020): 05C31, 05C35, 05C69
