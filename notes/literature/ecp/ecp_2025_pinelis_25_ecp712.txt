                                                   Abstract

       It is shown that Bernstein-type conditions on independent random variables are
       preserved by their sum. Some optimality properties of such preservation are proved.

       Keywords: Bernstein conditions; Bernstein inequalities; independent random variables.
       MSC2020 subject classifications: 60E15.
       Submitted to ECP on December 18, 2024, final version accepted on July 24, 2025.

    Writing, as usual,

    Bernstein-type conditions on random variables are closely related to the so-called
sub-exponentiality condition – see e.g. [9, Proposition 2.7.1] or [7, Proposition A]. The
sub-exponentiality property of the summands Xi is preserved by their sum Sn – say,
because any Orlicz norm is a norm. So, Bernstein-type properties of the Xi ’s should in
some form be preserved by their sum Sn .

and demonstrate corresponding optimality properties. The main results here are the
following two theorems.
