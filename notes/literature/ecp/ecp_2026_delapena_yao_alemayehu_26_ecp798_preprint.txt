1.2    Definitions and constructions of decoupled sequences
    In this subsection, we briefly provide the framework of tangent decoupling. From now on, we use the no-
tations di ’s and ei ’s to represent the possibly dependent random variables and their corresponding decoupled
random variables (see Definitions 1.1, 1.2 and 1.3). We denote L(X|F ) as the conditional law (distribution)
of a random variable X on a σ-algebra F.
Definition 1.1. Let {di }, {ei } be two sequences of random variables adapted to an increasing sequence of
σ-algebras {Fi }. Then {di } is said to be tangent to {ei } with respect to {Fi } if for all i, L(di |Fi−1 ) =
L(ei |Fi−1 ), i.e., the conditional distributions of di given Fi−1 and ei given Fi−1 are the same.
Definition 1.2. A sequence {ei } of random variables adapted to an increasing sequence of σ-algebra {Fi }
contained in F is said to be conditionally independent (CI) if there exists a σ-algebra G contained in F
such that {ei } is conditionally independent given G and L(ei |Fi−1 ) = L(ei |G).
Definition 1.3. Let {di } be an arbitrary sequence of random variables, then a conditionally independent
sequence {ei } which is also tangent to {di } will be called a decoupled version of {di }.
   The following proposition provided by Kwapien and Woiczynski (1989, [13]) guarantees that we can
always construct a decoupled sequence, with any possibly dependent sequence {di }.
Proposition 1.4. For any sequence of random variables {di }, one can find a decoupled sequence {ei } (on
a possibly enlarged probability space) which is tangent to the original sequence and in addition conditionally
independent given a master σ-algebra G. Frequently G = σ({di }).
   Specifically, with {di }, a tangent sequence w.r.t. Fi = σ(d1 , ..., di ) can be obtained via the following
steps:
   • First, we take d1 and e1 to be two independent copies of the same random mechanism.
   • With (d1 , .., di−1 ), the i-th pair of variables di and ei comes from i.i.d. copies of the same random
       mechanism given Fi−1 .
And ei ’s are conditionally independent w.r.t. G = Fn .

5     Concluding Remarks
    This paper is concerned with the application of decoupling inequalities, with a focus on the second
moments and variances of sums of dependent random variables. By deriving sharp bounds, we present
a robust framework that generalizes classical results — such as those of de la Pena and Hitczenko — to
a wider range of dependence structures, notably without requiring the non-negativity assumption for the
upper bound.
    Our findings demonstrate that precise upper bounds can be obtained under the minimal condition of
square integrability. These inequalities not only deepen theoretical understanding but also yield practical
tools for bounding tail probabilities via Chebyshev- and Paley-Zygmund-type inequalities in the presence of
dependence.
    We further highlight the applicability of these decoupling techniques to randomly stopped sums, show-
casing their effectiveness in settings where traditional martingale methods may be inadequate, particularly


                                                                            9
in the analysis of non-centered or heavy-tailed variables.
    By synthesizing existing literature and extending foundational inequalities, this work offers a meaningful
contribution to the study of dependent random variables. Future research may build upon this foundation
to explore applications in statistical inference and machine learning, where dependence among data points
is often intrinsic.
