# Finite Poisson--binomial novelty database audit

Date: 2026-07-16
Cutoff: 2026-07-16
Status: reproducible public-database and citation-chain audit; all plausible overlaps checked in primary full text; authenticated MathSciNet search unavailable

## Verdict

No prior theorem found in the accessible indexed literature implies

\[
V\left(1-\frac{f_{D-1}f_{D+1}}{f_D^2}\right)\ge \frac14
\]

for every finite nondegenerate Poisson--binomial law with `V >= 1` at its first nonterminal strict descent `D`.

The result is best classified as:

> **apparently new after a documented multi-database and citation-chain search**.

The publication-safe sentence remains:

> We are not aware of a previous finite variance-normalized bound for the three-mass curvature at the first strict descent of a Poisson--binomial law.

Do not call this a proved historical first. The current environment could not enter an authenticated MathSciNet result session, and bibliographic databases demonstrably disagree about citation coverage. Confidence in the cautious claim is **moderate--high**; confidence sufficient for an unqualified priority claim is not available.

The exact prior ingredients are known and must be credited:

- Hillion--Johnson's cubic coefficient inequalities;
- Bobkov--Marsiglietti--Melbourne's lattice max-atom/variance inequality.

What the search did not find elsewhere is the endpoint-aware curvature propagation, the resulting two-sided modal mass windows, and their synthesis with variance to produce a universal finite `1/4` bound at the first strict descent.

## Target used for overlap decisions

An alleged overlap had to match or imply all of the following.

| Feature | Target requirement |
|---|---|
| Population | finite sum of independent, not necessarily identically distributed Bernoulli variables |
| Location | first strict descent, equivalently just after the largest modal index |
| Quantity | normalized three-mass deficit `1-f_(D-1)f_(D+1)/f_D^2` |
| Scale | original variance `V` of the same law |
| Regime | finite, nonasymptotic, including boundary-adjacent cases |
| Constant | universal lower bound `1/4` when `V >= 1` |

Results about mode location, a single modal atom, qualitative log-concavity, summand-count-normalized Newton inequalities, tails, entropy, or asymptotic local limits were retained as near hits but not counted as theorem overlaps.

## The closest three-mass result: ULC(n)

The standard `ULC(n)` estimate, recorded for example by [Hillion--Johnson](https://doi.org/10.1214/14-AOP973), Definition 3.11 and equation (41), and by [Marsiglietti--Melbourne](https://doi.org/10.1093/imrn/rnag023), after Definition 1.3, is

\[
f_k^2\ge
\left(1+\frac1{n-k}\right)
\left(1+\frac1k\right)f_{k-1}f_{k+1},
\]

which gives

\[
\delta_k
=1-\frac{f_{k-1}f_{k+1}}{f_k^2}
\ge \frac{n+1}{(k+1)(n-k+1)}.
\]

This is the closest standard quantitative inequality for the same three masses found in the screened literature. It is normalized by the number of Bernoulli factors, not by variance, and it does not imply the target.

An explicit nonimplication test is useful. Take `m` Bernoulli variables with parameter `epsilon_m` and `m` with parameter `1-epsilon_m`, where

\[
\epsilon_m(1-\epsilon_m)=\frac1{2m}.
\]

Then `V=1`, `n=2m`, symmetry puts the first strict descent at `D=m+1`, and the displayed ULC bound supplies only

\[
\delta_D\ge\frac{2m+1}{m(m+2)}\longrightarrow0.
\]

The target requires `delta_D >= 1/4` in this regime. Thus a paper containing only the `ULC(n)` estimate has not already proved the variance-scale result by routine substitution.

## Remaining-candidate full-text exclusions

### Fontana--Semeraro

[The Bernoulli structure of discrete distributions](https://doi.org/10.1214/25-ECP741) ([arXiv version](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.13920)) studies the polytope of joint, generally dependent Bernoulli vectors having a prescribed sum distribution. Its main results describe the fiber's extreme points, cross-moments, volume measure, and induced Dirichlet law. Independence is not characterized inside the fiber. Poisson--binomial laws occur only as background examples, and there is no variance/adjacent-mass/descent theorem.

This scope distinction is essential: an arbitrary finite distribution can be represented as a sum of **dependent** Bernoullis, so those representation results cannot transfer Poisson--binomial curvature.

### Brazitikos--Pafis

[Discrete log-concavity and threshold phenomena for atomic measures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15444) defines ordinary one-dimensional discrete log-concavity by `p_k^2 >= p_(k-1)p_(k+1)`. Its theorems concern integrable log-concave extensions, Cramér-transform moments, and random-polytope threshold phenomena. They provide only `delta_k >= 0` at the coefficient level and do not address a mode or adjacent-drop magnitude.

### Recent anti-concentration and degree-of-freedom papers

- [Gaxiola--Melbourne--Pigno--Pollard](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05793), Theorems 1.3/4.4 and 1.4/4.5, bound variance using one selected atom or the maximum atom. No adjacent masses occur.
- [Jakimiuk--Murawski--Nayar--Słobodianiuk](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.disc.2024.114020), Theorems 1--2, optimize single-atom/variance quantities for discrete log-concave or integral-mean ULC laws.
- [Aravinda](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.disc.2023.113683), Theorem 1.4, gives `M^(-2) >= 1+V`, again a one-atom inequality.
- [Marsiglietti--Melbourne's Littlewood--Offord note](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25869), Theorem 1.1 and Proposition 3.4, gives weighted-sum and Poisson--binomial maximum-atom bounds, not local ratios.

These papers strengthen the global anti-concentration side of the proof ecosystem. None supplies the missing first-descent curvature.

## Publication recommendation

Retain the current provenance split:

1. explicitly attribute Hillion--Johnson's cubic inequalities;
2. explicitly attribute Bobkov--Marsiglietti--Melbourne's max-atom/variance bound;
3. claim novelty only for the finite first-descent propagation-to-variance synthesis;
4. use “we are not aware of” rather than “the first” or “new” without qualification;
5. run one authenticated MathSciNet query family immediately before submission if access becomes available, and record the date in this note.
