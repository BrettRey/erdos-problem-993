# Jerrum--Patel Lemma 17: proof-scope audit

Date: 2026-07-16
Status: primary-source audit; no numerical root finding used

## Bottom line

Jerrum and Patel's positive-axis obstruction survives, but their proof does not establish the exact family printed in Lemma 17 when the subdivision parameter is positive.

- The sentence `Delta = 2, H_2 = <f_1,f_2>` is a definite index error. In Buys's notation the correct objects are `Delta = 3` and `H_3 = <f_1,f_2>`. This correction is harmless for the maximum-degree-three conclusion.
- Buys's Lemma 13 supplies some level-balanced maximum-degree-three tree. After the periodic word in the application is tracked through the proof, one obtains a finite union of endpoint-phase truncations of that periodic family. This is enough to keep consecutive branching levels separated and hence enough for Jerrum--Patel's `H`-free zero-region obstruction.
- It does not force both endpoints to align with the single family of complete binary trees whose every edge is subdivided exactly `2k` times. For `k >= 1`, the exact-family assertion in Lemma 17 is therefore **not established by the cited argument**. It is not disproved.
- For `k = 0` there is only one periodic phase, and the argument reduces to the ordinary complete-binary-tree family.

The same language appears in the published version, so this is not a stale-arXiv issue.

## Exact statement and encoding

For a fixed `k`, Jerrum--Patel define `T_d` by starting from a complete binary tree of height `d` and inserting `2k` degree-two vertices into every edge. With

```text
f_1(z) = lambda/(1+z),
f_2(z) = lambda/(1+z)^2,
g = f_2 o f_1^(2k),
```

the root occupation ratio is

```text
g^d(lambda) = (g^d o f_1)(0).
```

Using the convention that the word `d_1 ... d_m` represents

```text
f_{d_m} o ... o f_{d_1}(0),
```

the exact family has word

```text
1 (1^(2k) 2)^d.
```

Thus the printed Lemma 17 needs zeros from this fixed start phase and fixed end phase, not merely from some word with the same periodic interior.

## The index issue

Buys defines, for `Delta >= 3`,

```text
H_Delta = <f_1, ..., f_(Delta-1)>.
```

Consequently `f_1` and `f_2` generate `H_3`. Jerrum--Patel's proof says instead that `Delta = 2` and `H_2` is generated by `f_1,f_2`. Under the cited definitions this is false.

There is a related type slip in the same passage: `H_Delta` is Buys's semigroup of maps, while his tree class is `G_Delta`. Thus maps belong to `H_3`; the trees output by Lemma 4 and Lemma 13 belong to `G_3`, not to `H_2` or `H_3`.

The repair is local:

```text
Delta = 3, H_3 = <f_1,f_2>.
```

Buys's Lemma 4 then converts the relevant semigroup word into a tree of maximum graph degree three, exactly as the surrounding Jerrum--Patel argument requires. No downstream numerical constant or forbidden-subgraph choice changes.

Classification: **correctable notation/index error**.

## What Buys's Lemma 13 actually gives

Buys's Lemma 13 says that if `g in H_Delta` is not a pure iterate of `f_1` and `g_lambda0` has an indifferent fixed point, then every neighbourhood of `lambda_0` contains a parameter that is a zero of the independence polynomial of **some** tree in `G_Delta`.

Its proof proceeds as follows.

1. A marked critical orbit gives a map `h in H_Delta` for which the family `(g^n o h)(0)` is non-normal.
2. Montel gives a nearby parameter at which this expression hits `-1`, `0`, or infinity.
3. In the latter two cases the proof removes one or two **outer** generators and then applies Lemma 4.

The statement of Lemma 13 does not identify the output with a prescribed periodic family. Buys's Remark 8 says that a critical orbit can be rewritten as `h(0)` for a semigroup element `h`; its printed statement does not say that `h` is the particular one-period initial segment asserted by Jerrum--Patel.

Tracking the critical orbit for `g = f_2 o f_1^(2k)` does preserve the cyclic generator order. After complete periods are absorbed into the outer `g^n`, the resulting tree word is a contiguous finite subword of the bi-infinite periodic word

```text
... 1^(2k) 2 1^(2k) 2 1^(2k) 2 ... .
```

The final Montel reduction can change the outer endpoint by zero, one, or two generators. This reconstruction therefore supports a finite collection of phase-truncated periodic-word families. It does not select the exact word `1 (1^(2k) 2)^d`.

Classification: **the broader phase-truncated conclusion is repairable by spelling out the word tracking; it is stronger than the literal statement of Remark 8 but follows from the structure of its proof**.

## Why the main obstruction survives

Every reconstructed word uses branching factor two only at the periodic `2` positions, with `2k` unary levels between successive internal branching levels. Endpoint truncation cannot create two internal branching levels closer together. The trees remain maximum-degree three.

Those are the two properties used after Lemma 17:

- if the excluded graph `H` has a vertex of degree at least four, maximum degree three excludes it;
- if `H` has two degree-three vertices, choosing `k` above their relevant separation keeps the constructed branching vertices sufficiently far apart.

Accordingly, the proof still shows that the required `H`-free class has zeros accumulating at a positive real activity. What should be widened is the displayed tree family, not the zero-region conclusion.

## What remains open for the exact family

For `k >= 1`, neither Buys's Lemma 13 and Remark 8 nor Jerrum--Patel's endpoint argument proves that nearby hits occur for

```text
(g^d o f_1)(0) = -1,
```

equivalently for the exact uniformly subdivided `T_d`. A repair must control **both** endpoint phases. Sufficient new inputs would include either:

- a phase-alignment theorem forcing `h` to equal `f_1` modulo complete `g` blocks and ruling out the endpoint deletions; or
- a transfer theorem carrying zero accumulation from one fixed phase-truncated periodic family to the exact endpoint-aligned family.

The current sources provide neither. Failure of the citation chain is not a counterexample to the exact-family statement.

Classification: **open proof-scope gap for the printed exact-family assertion when `k >= 1`**.

## Safe citation language

Publication-safe wording for this project is:

> Jerrum and Patel construct maximum-degree-three balanced tree families with branching levels separated by arbitrarily long unary stretches whose independence-polynomial zeros accumulate on the positive real axis. Their Lemma 17 states this for the single endpoint-aligned family of uniformly subdivided complete binary trees. The cited Buys argument directly supports a finite union of phase-truncated periodic families; that broader conclusion suffices for their `H`-free zero-region obstruction. The exact endpoint-aligned statement requires an additional phase-control argument when the subdivision length is positive.

Do not write that Lemma 17 is false. Do not cite it without the family-scope caveat when the exact uniformly subdivided family matters.
