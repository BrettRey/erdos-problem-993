     This paper concerns the class Wp introduced by Staples [32, 33]. Well-covered graphs
originate in the work of Plummer [29, 30]; very well-covered graphs were introduced by
Favaron [7], and structural results for special well-covered families include [8, 34]. A graph
is in Wp if every p pairwise disjoint independent sets can be enlarged to p pairwise disjoint
maximum independent sets. The class W1 is precisely the class of well-covered graphs.
The class W2 is especially important because it coincides, for graphs without isolated
vertices, with the class of 1-well-covered graphs [33, 25]; related W2 subclasses were
studied by Pinter [28]. These graph classes also occur naturally in edge-ideal theory and
Gorenstein graph theory [35, 14]. On the enumerative side, counterexamples, roller-coaster
phenomena and positive results for very well-covered graphs appear in [27, 21, 24, 23, 4].
Corona constructions, introduced by Frucht and Harary [9], provide a large source of
well-covered and Wp examples. In a recent paper, Hoang, Levit, Mandrescu and Pham
studied quasi-regularizability and log-concavity for Wp graphs and proved the p-quasi-
regularizability threshold for connected Wp graphs when p ̸= 2 [12]. They left the following
two questions.
Problem 4.1 (Hoang–Levit–Mandrescu–Pham). What conditions on the Wp graph G
guarantee that the independence polynomial I(G; x) is log-concave or at least unimodal?
Conjecture 4.2 (Hoang–Levit–Mandrescu–Pham). Let G be a connected W2 graph. Then
G is 2-quasi-regularizable if and only if

                                      n(G) ≥ 3α(G).

   The exclusion of p = 2 in the known threshold is not cosmetic. The cycle C5 belongs
to W2 but is not 2-quasi-regularizable: if S is a maximum independent set, then |S| = 2
and |N (S)| = 3 < 4. This is exactly the failure of the inequality n(C5 ) ≥ 3α(C5 ); see
Figure 1. The main result below proves that this is the only kind of failure.

   Main structural theorem. Let G be a connected graph in W2 . If A is an
   independent set which is not maximum, then

                                      |NG (A)| ≥ 2|A|.

   Consequently, a connected W2 graph is 2-quasi-regularizable if and only if n(G) ≥
   3α(G).

    The proof is local. If a smallest counterexample A existed, then |N (A)| = 2|A| − 1.
Each vertex of A would have a unique private neighbor; all other neighbors would see
at least two vertices of A; private neighbors would be forced away from the localization
GA ; every non-private vertex touching GA would be universal on A; and finally a second
localization would build an augmenting set that contradicts the W2 avoidance property.
Figures 4–7 show the successive reductions.

part of the paper. The coefficient criteria in Section 3 are included to make the consequence
for Problem 4.1 self-contained: they combine known two-sided coefficient inequalities with

2. Preliminaries
    All graphs considered in this paper are finite, undirected, and simple. For undefined
graph-theoretic terminology we refer the reader to Diestel [6]; for matching terminology
we refer to Lovász and Plummer [26].
    We write V (G) and E(G) for the vertex set and the edge set, n(G) = |V (G)|, and α(G)
for the independence number. The family of independent sets is denoted by Ind(G), and
the family of maximum independent sets by Ω(G). For A ⊆ V (G), the open neighborhood
is
                NG (A) = {v ∈ V (G) − A : uv ∈ E(G) for some u ∈ A},
with NG [A] = A ∪ NG (A). When A is independent, the localization of G at A is
                                            GA = G − NG [A].
For A = {v} we write Gv . Figure 2 illustrates the localization operation.
Definition 2.1 (Wp graphs). Let p ≥ 1. A graph G belongs to Wp if n(G) ≥ p and,
for every p pairwise disjoint independent sets A1 , . . . , Ap , there exist p pairwise disjoint
maximum independent sets S1 , . . . , Sp ∈ Ω(G) such that Ai ⊆ Si for every i.
Definition 2.2 (Quasi-regularizability). Let λ > 0. A graph G is λ-quasi-regularizable if
                                             λ|A| ≤ |NG (A)|
for every independent set A of G. The case λ = 1 is the usual notion of quasi-regularizability
due to Berge [2].

Lemma 2.4 (Keilson–Gerber product lemma). Let P (x) and Q(x) have nonnegative
coefficients and no internal zero coefficients. If P (x) is log-concave and Q(x) is unimodal,
then P (x)Q(x) is unimodal. If both P (x) and Q(x) are log-concave, then P (x)Q(x) is
log-concave.

   The lemma is the standard polynomial form of a convolution theorem for nonnegative
sequences. Keilson and Gerber established the classical strong-unimodality principle
behind the first assertion [18]; in the present nonnegative setting one must include the
no-internal-zero condition, as in the corrected formulation of Levit and Mandrescu [19,
Theorem 1.2 and Corollary 1.3]. The compact independence-polynomial wording also
appears explicitly in [22, Theorem 1] and is the form quoted by Hoang–Levit–Mandrescu–
Pham [12, Lemma 1.4]. Since independence polynomials have strictly positive coefficients
from degree 0 through degree α(G), the hypothesis is automatic in all applications below.

unions of maximum independent sets of the components, and restricting the defining
property to one component gives the converse.

and x ∈/ S. Then y ∈  / S, and hence S ∪ {x} is independent, contradicting the maximality

4. A local expansion theorem for connected W2 graphs
   This section proves the result that resolves Conjecture 4.2. The proof is organized as
a minimal-counterexample argument. The forbidden normal form is drawn in Figure 4.

Theorem 4.1 (Local neighborhood expansion). Let G be a connected graph in W2 . If
A ∈ Ind(G) is not maximum, then

                                           |NG (A)| ≥ 2|A|.

5. Solution of Conjecture 4.2
Theorem 5.1 (Conjecture 4.2). Let G be a connected graph in W2 . Then G is 2-quasi-
regularizable if and only if
                                 n(G) ≥ 3α(G).

Proof. If G is 2-quasi-regularizable and S ∈ Ω(G), then

                          n(G) − α(G) = |NG (S)| ≥ 2|S| = 2α(G),

so n(G) ≥ 3α(G).
   Conversely, assume n(G) ≥ 3α(G) and let A ∈ Ind(G). If A is not maximum, then
Theorem 4.1 gives
                                |NG (A)| ≥ 2|A|.
If A is maximum, then

                          |NG (A)| = n(G) − α(G) ≥ 2α(G) = 2|A|.

Thus |NG (A)| ≥ 2|A| for every independent set A, which is precisely 2-quasi-regularizability.


Remark 5.2. Theorem 4.1 is stronger than Conjecture 4.2. The global inequality n(G) ≥
3α(G) is needed only for maximum independent sets. Every non-maximum independent
set in a connected W2 graph satisfies the 2-quasi-regularizability inequality automatically.

7. Final remarks
    The local theorem explains why the case p = 2 was delicate. In a smallest hypothetical
counterexample, localizing at A − {x} creates a K2 component {x, mx }. Such a component
is allowed in W2 , whereas higher p arguments usually benefit from stronger local degree
constraints. The proof above eliminates precisely this obstruction by showing that the
remaining connection to GA must pass through a unique universal vertex, and then using
the W2 extension property one final time.
    For independence polynomials, the conclusion is that the conditions in Problem 4.1 can
now be applied uniformly at the quasi-regularizability threshold: for p ̸= 2 by the theorem
of Hoang–Levit–Mandrescu–Pham, and for p = 2 by Theorem 5.1. Componentwise versions
follow from Lemma 2.4, which is why log-concavity results for connected components
extend cleanly to disconnected Wp graphs.
